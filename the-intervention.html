The Intervention

Between the Lines seeks to reclaim the administrative systems of education institutions.

Much like the UK border regime co-opts civilians and public service workers as unofficial enforcers, Between the Lines relies on the collaboration of frontline administrative staff to embed the lived experiences of international students back into the administrative systems that govern them.

Stories and experiences from international students who have been impacted by the influence of the UK border control system on Higher Education institutions were collected and compiled into a text document.

The text document was converted into binary data (1's and 0's).

The document was sent to Twist Bioscience, who's Scientists encoded the binary data into DNA nucleotides and synthesised DNA strands according to the sequence produced.

Synthetic DNA is sent back in dried form inside a small, sealed metal capsule.

The dried DNA is extracted from the metal capsules and added to writing ink.

The ink is injected into clear, acrylic pens.

The pens are posted to the administration hubs of every Higher Education provider who is a registered Student Sponsor.

The pens are received by administration staff, along with a letter inviting them to be the new keeper of the pen, should they choose to accept the responsibilities it carries.

The new Keeper is instructed to use the pen when undertaking administrative tasks on behalf of their institution.

DNA within the ink transfers to the paper and the lived experiences of those impacted permeate the administrative systems that govern them.
