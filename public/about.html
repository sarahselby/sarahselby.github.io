About the Project

Between the Lines is a creative intervention responding to the lived experiences of international students who have been impacted by the influence of the UK border control system on Higher Education Institutions. The project uses DNA data storage technology to embed stories from the community back into the administrative systems that govern them.

DNA data storage is an emerging technology that allows digital information to be encoded into laboratory created DNA. Much like we can store data on memory sticks or the cloud, DNA can also be used as a digital storage medium. The resulting synthetic DNA can be preserved for thousands of years, and the digital data can be retrieved via DNA sequencing.

In this instance, the synthetic DNA has been embedded into writing ink and contains the written testimony of students who have faced difficult immigration practices, even from inside the UK higher education institutions in which they are studying.
